Early Labor (Latent Phase)
Cervix dilates 0–6cm. Contractions 5–20 min apart, 30–45 seconds each. Can last 6–12 hours for first-time moms.
Stay home, stay calm, eat light, rest
Start timing contractions with the timer
Call your doctor if water breaks
2
Active Labor
Cervix dilates 6–10cm. Contractions 3–5 min apart, 45–60 sec. This is when you go to the hospital.
5-1-1 rule: contractions every 5min, 1min long, for 1 hour
Pain relief options: epidural, breathing, movement
Partner's role: breathing coach, ice chips, support
3
Transition & Pushing
Cervix fully dilated at 10cm. Intense contractions 2–3 min apart. Pushing phase: 20 min to 2 hours.
Hardest but shortest phase — you're almost there
Pushing techniques: purple pushing vs breathing down
Episiotomy vs tearing — discuss with your doctor
🚨 Call your doctor immediately if:
💧
Water breaks before 37 weeks
🩸
Heavy bleeding at any time
👶
Baby not moving for 2+ hours
🤕
Severe headache + vision changes
