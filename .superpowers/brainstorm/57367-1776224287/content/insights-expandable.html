This week's birth focus
Understanding Labor Signs
Braxton Hicks · water breaking · real contractions
▾
Braxton Hicks — irregular practice contractions, not painful, no pattern. Normal from week 20+.
Water breaking — can be a trickle or gush. Call your doctor immediately. Don't wait for contractions.
Real labor — contractions are regular, get stronger, closer together, and don't stop when you move.

Stage 1
Early signs & latent labor
Cervix 0–6cm · 5–20 min apart · stay home
▾
Cervix dilates 0–6cm. Contractions every 5–20 min, lasting 30–45 sec. Can last 6–12 hours for first-time moms.
What to do: Stay home. Eat light, stay hydrated, rest between contractions. Watch a movie, take a bath.
Partner role: Time contractions, keep things calm, prepare the hospital bag, be on standby.

Stage 2
Active labor
Cervix 6–10cm · 5-1-1 rule → go to hospital
▾
Contractions every 3–5 min, lasting 45–60 sec. Intense, regular, can't talk through them.
5-1-1 rule: contractions every 5 min, lasting 1 min, for at least 1 hour → head to hospital.
Pain relief: Epidural available now. Also: breathing techniques, movement, warm shower, TENS.
Partner role: Breathing coach, counter-pressure on back, eye contact, encouragement.

Stage 3
Transition & pushing
10cm · hardest + shortest · 20 min – 2h pushing
▾
Cervix fully dilated. Most intense contractions — 2–3 min apart. Usually 15–60 min for transition.
Pushing: Your doctor guides you. Breathe down vs purple pushing — ask your OB which they prefer.
You're almost there. This is the hardest part but it doesn't last long.
👶
Stage 4
Birth & golden hour
Skin-to-skin · cord clamping · first feed
▾
Skin-to-skin immediately — regulates baby's temperature, heartbeat, and starts bonding.
Delayed cord clamping — waiting 1–3 min transfers extra blood and stem cells to baby.
First feed — colostrum is ready. It's liquid gold, even a few drops matters.
Placenta delivery — usually within 30 min. Mild contractions continue.
🌸
Stage 5
Recovery & postpartum
First 6 weeks · body · emotions · feeding
▾
Physical recovery: Bleeding (lochia) up to 6 weeks. Perineal care. Rest is not optional.
Baby blues vs PPD: Mood dips are normal for 2 weeks. Beyond that, talk to your doctor.
6-week checkup: Book before you deliver. Incision check, contraception, mental health screening.
